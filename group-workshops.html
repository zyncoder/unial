FAQ's

Frequently asked question

Group workshops are interactive training sessions where participants engage in activities and discussions to learn and develop specific skills or knowledge areas. They are beneficial because they foster collaboration, enhance problem-solving skills, encourage peer learning, and provide opportunities for hands-on practice in a supportive environment.

Preparation for a group workshop involves several steps: identifying the objectives and outcomes, selecting relevant materials and activities, arranging the logistics (such as venue, equipment, and refreshments), inviting participants, and preparing any pre-workshop readings or tasks. It's also helpful to create a detailed agenda to ensure the workshop runs smoothly.

Group workshops often include a variety of activities such as icebreakers to build rapport, group discussions for idea sharing, role-playing for practical application, case studies for problem-solving, and collaborative projects to foster teamwork. These activities are designed to be interactive and engaging to maximize learning and participation.

The success of a group workshop can be measured through various means, including participant feedback surveys, pre- and post-workshop assessments to gauge learning, observation of participant engagement during activities, and follow-up evaluations to see how well participants apply the skills or knowledge in their work. Analyzing these metrics helps to understand the workshop's impact and areas for improvement.
